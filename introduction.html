Please read this Explanatory Statement in full before deciding whether or not to participate in this research. If you would like further information regarding any aspect of this project, you are encouraged to contact the researchers via the email addresses listed in the footer.

What does the research involve?

The aim of this research is to compare the effectiveness of different visualisation methods to show structural characteristics of the underlying graphs. In order to help us with this evaluation you will be asked to complete this online survey. You will go through the following steps:

  1. answer three short questions concerning your past experience with network diagrams.
  2. read about the visual representations and characteristics of networks.
  3. shown a few examples of each type of diagram.
  4. answer a few questions.

The time you take to answer each question and your answers will be recorded. An eye tracker will track your eye movement. We estimate that you will need about 30 minutes to complete the online survey.

Why were you chosen for this research?

You responded to our post on Monash Memo and/or an email and/or a social media post advertising this research.

Consenting to participate in the project and withdrawing from the research

Participation in this study is entirely voluntary and you are under no obligation to complete the survey. If you proceed with this survey, your consent will be assumed granted.

You can withdraw from the study by exiting the survey without completing all questions. If you choose to provide your contact details, you can also withdraw your completed survey response by request with the project investigators. If you complete the survey anonymously, your response cannot be withdrawn as we have no way of identifying which was your response.

Possible benefits and risks to participants

The study is low-risk and it is not anticipated that there will be any negative impact on participants.

Payment

At the end of the survey, you will have the opportunity to provide us with your contact details. If you choose to do so, you will then have a chance to win a prize of $50. After the study is completed (within about 3 months), we will award the $50 prize to a randomly chosen participant as thanks for their participation.

Confidentiality

Your identity will be kept confidential.

Storage of data

Storage of the data collected will adhere to the University regulations and kept on University premises in a locked cupboard/filing cabinet for 5 years, and/or on a secure computer. A report of the study may be submitted for publication, but individual participants will not be identifiable in such a report.

Results

Results from the survey may be published as a journal article or conference paper or in book chapter.

Results from the survey may be included in the PhD thesis of Vahan Yoghourdjian (student investigator).

Published results will be in totally de-identified summary form.
